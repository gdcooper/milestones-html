Basic Tags

A New Tag: The Paragraph

In HTML, paragraphs are blocks of text typically placed in the 'body' section of the code. They denote the 'body' text of a website, hence the placement. A paragraph is used to create easy blocks of text to manipulate and put between headings, and other things like lists or pictures. It is denoted in HTML using p.

Blockquotes!

Blockquotes are used to cite other quotes in a differing format, often focusing on longer quotes and quotes from other websites.

"Always remember that you are absolutely unique. Just like everyone else."

Pre-Formatted Text

        This tag, pre, is used to denote pre-formatted text that is already spaced, and will stay at a consistent width and spacing no matter what.

Emphasis: The em and i Tags

In HTML, there are two different ways to emphasize text. There is the em tag, which looks like this: Shouting is fun!

Then, there is also the i tag, which both emphasizes the text and sets it off from the rest: Shouting is fun!

Bolding: The b and strong Tags

There are also ways to bold text, or make it bold. The b tag is stylistic, and makes text bold: Shouting is fun!

However, there is also the strong tag, which was made as part of original html and just makes text stronger than the surroundings: Shouting is fun!

This is the same relationship betwen i and em: One was original, intended for function, and the other evolved for style.
